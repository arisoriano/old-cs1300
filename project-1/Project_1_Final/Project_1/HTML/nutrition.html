Thoeretically, losing weight is simple: burn more calories than you take in. Yet if it were that easy, then the millions of people looking to shed pounds would all be skinny. Having said that, lets look at some simple math for a second.

Weight Loss Math

If you didn't know, one pound of human body weight is approximately equal to 3,500 calories. So in order to lose one pound you must have a net loss of 3,500 calories. We can easily see this in the following equation:

Caloric Intake (Calories Eaten) - Caloric Output (Calories Burned) = Net Calories

As you can see from the formula there can either be a positve or negative net calories. You always want a negative number of net calories, indicating loss of calories and hence weight. A positve amount, obviously means you gained weight, by eating more than you burned.

Glycemic Index of Foods

What is the Glycemic Index, essentially it how well your blood absorbs and spreads sugar out throughout the body. Some foods give your circulatory system a hard time or "higher load" due to more carbohydrates and sugars and work. Generally you want to consume low glycemic foods to avoid hunger spikes and overeating.
